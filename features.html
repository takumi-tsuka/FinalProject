origin of UDON

Udon is said to have originated in China. There is a theory that it was introduced via the Silk Road to the west as pasta and then to Japan as udon. It is believed to have changed depending on the local environment.

how did it come?

There is a theory that udon was introduced from China during the Nara period (710-794) and was called "udon" after a snack called "konton". Another popular theory is that a Japanese Buddhist monk named Kukai went to China to study and introduced udon to the Chinese people when he returned.

how did it change?

It is said that before the Edo period(1603-1867), udon was shaped like crushed dumplings, but it is believed that during the Edo period, udon took its present string-like form. A study of udon in various parts of Japan shows that many of them took on their current udon shape during the Edo period.

Modern Udon

Tempura with various ingredients, egg and egg topping, etc. were developed in the mid to late Edo period, and are still loved by the Japanese as a food for the common people, unchanged from the Edo period.

noodles of UDON

Udon noodles, as mentioned above, are made from high-quality wheat, pure water, and mineral-rich salt. Udon noodles are eaten all over Japan, but most regions, especially famous ones, have these conditions in place. Udon noodles are very easy to digest, and it is said that eating udon noodles when you have a cold or are sick will make you feel better.
